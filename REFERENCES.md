## Methodology Notes

### Expert System Rule Development

The rules in the MARX system were developed using a combination of:

1. **Academic Research**: Peer-reviewed journals in marketing, business strategy, and consumer behavior
2. **Industry Reports**: Data from marketing platforms (HubSpot, Google, Meta), research firms (Gartner, Forrester), and industry associations
3. **Benchmark Data**: Aggregated performance data from marketing platforms and agencies
4. **Best Practices**: Established frameworks from marketing thought leaders and practitioners
5. **Empirical Validation**: Cross-referenced with multiple sources to ensure reliability

### Data Currency

- All benchmarks and statistics reflect 2023-2024 data where available
- Historical references (Porter 1980, Henderson 1970) used for enduring strategic frameworks
- Industry standards updated annually from authoritative sources

### Rule Confidence Levels

Rules are based on sources with varying levels of evidence:
- **High Confidence**: Multiple academic studies + industry consensus (e.g., CLV > 3x CAC)
- **Medium Confidence**: Industry reports + platform data (e.g., specific budget allocations)
- **Contextual**: Best practices that vary by industry (e.g., content frequency recommendations)
